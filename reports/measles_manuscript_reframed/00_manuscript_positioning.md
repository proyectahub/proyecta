# Manuscript Positioning

## Selected Title

Globally conserved measles virus therapeutic pockets define a structure-guided repurposing framework in the context of re-emergence in Mexico and Latin America

## Alternative Titles

1. Structure-guided prioritization of globally conserved measles virus therapeutic pockets for antiviral repurposing
2. Global conservation of measles virus F and polymerase pockets supports a rational antiviral repurposing strategy

## Central Hypothesis

Despite heterogeneous regional surveillance and lineage turnover, measles virus preserves structurally actionable small-molecule pockets in prefusion F and the L polymerase complex that remain sufficiently conserved across regional and global sequence space to support rational antiviral repurposing. The recent re-emergence of measles in Mexico and Latin America provides the epidemiologic rationale for this search, but therapeutic target definition should be anchored in a broader regional-global comparative framework rather than in local sequence density alone.

## Overall Objective

To identify and prioritize conserved, structurally defined measles virus therapeutic pockets and to establish a docking-ready repurposing framework grounded in curated regional and global sequence evidence.

## Specific Aims

1. Define the epidemiologic and surveillance rationale for antiviral preparedness by positioning the recent resurgence in Mexico and Latin America as the regional context for a broader therapeutic search.
2. Curate a regional-global measles virus sequence framework and quantify the conservation of docking-relevant residues in the prefusion F pocket and the L polymerase pocket.
3. Translate structure and conservation evidence into a therapeutic repurposing workflow that prioritizes L as the primary docking campaign, F as the orthogonal secondary campaign, and explicit structural comparators as pocket validators.

## Editorial Positioning

The manuscript should be framed around three linked pillars:

1. global conservation of druggable pockets,
2. regional outbreak relevance,
3. therapeutic repurposing rationale.

Under this framing, Mexico is not removed from the manuscript; its role is narrowed and clarified. The Mexican and Latin American outbreak signal motivates the study and supports the surveillance-gap argument, but the main therapeutic claims are anchored in broader regional-global sequence evidence and in structurally defined F and L pockets.
