# Review Memo

## Scope

This review covers the Word figure compilation for the `global conservation of druggable pockets` pillar and evaluates whether the document is scientifically coherent, internally consistent, and ready to support manuscript assembly.

## Fixed During Review

1. The initial figure document used overly compressed result text and under-reported the key quantitative observations. This was corrected by expanding the figure-specific `Results Text` blocks for Figures 2-6.
2. Terminology was tightened to reduce drift between `L`, `L polymerase`, and `L-P`. The revised figure text now refers more consistently to the `L-P polymerase module` when the target is discussed as a therapeutic system rather than as an isolated chain.

## Remaining Findings

1. The figure Word document is suitable as a manuscript-compilation file, but it is not yet citation-complete. Inline citation callouts are still absent from the figure pages and will need to be reintroduced when the full manuscript is assembled.
2. Figure 4 is the mechanistic center of the pillar and already makes the right claim, but the Word document would become more reviewer-resistant if the conserved-residue counts and RMSD values were mirrored in a short figure-side bullet list or micro-table instead of appearing only in paragraph form.
3. Figure 2 appropriately states that no public Mexican genome-scale outbreak sequences were recovered in the fetched dataset, but final manuscript language should continue to avoid implying that such sequences do not exist anywhere outside public retrieval. The current phrasing is acceptable, but this distinction should be preserved carefully during later prose editing.
4. Figure 6 clearly separates repurposing candidates from structural comparators, but the full manuscript will need to state explicitly whether the translational library is limited to FDA-approved small molecules, clinically approved human drugs more broadly, or a curated mixed regulatory set.

## Scientific Appraisal

The sequence of Figures 2 through 6 is now scientifically coherent and substantially more publishable than the earlier Mexico-centered ordering. The document now supports the intended argument that regional outbreak relevance motivates the study, while global conservation and structural tractability carry the therapeutic claim. In particular, the progression from sequence evidence to target landscape, then to pocket conservation, then to prioritization, and finally to repurposing logic is appropriate for an international virology or antiviral-discovery audience.

## Verdict

The revised figure document is acceptable as a manuscript assembly artifact and now communicates the global-conservation pillar clearly. It is not yet the final submission-ready figure section, but it is strong enough to serve as the basis for the integrated manuscript Word draft.
