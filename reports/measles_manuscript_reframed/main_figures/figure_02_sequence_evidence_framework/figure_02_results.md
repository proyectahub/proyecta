Public sequence availability was asymmetric across analytic layers. After exact geographic curation, only 10 unique Mexican N-gene records were retained, whereas no Mexican F sequences and no Mexican L sequences were available in the curated dataset. By contrast, Latin American exact records yielded 138 N sequences, 10 F sequences, and 14 L sequences, while the global complete-genome context contributed 405 N, 432 F, and 630 L sequences. The latest curated Mexican public record dated to 2019, indicating that the 2025-2026 outbreak was not represented by publicly retrievable Mexican genome-scale data in the dataset fetched on June 6, 2026. These observations justified a regional-global analytical design in which outbreak relevance remained regional, but docking-oriented conservation required broader sequence support.
