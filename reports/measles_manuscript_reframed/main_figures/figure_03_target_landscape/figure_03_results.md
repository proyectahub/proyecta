Integration of viral function with public structural evidence converged on two actionable therapeutic axes: the prefusion F pocket and the polymerase-centered L-P machinery. Among the entry proteins, F uniquely provided a small-molecule-accessible, target-engaged prefusion cavity, whereas H was better supported as a receptor-context module than as a first-pass docking target. Within the replication module, the strongest target-engaged structural evidence was concentrated in ligand-bound polymerase assemblies that directly connect pocket architecture with antiviral mechanism. These features positioned the L-P polymerase module as the primary therapeutic axis and the prefusion F pocket as the leading orthogonal entry-focused target.
