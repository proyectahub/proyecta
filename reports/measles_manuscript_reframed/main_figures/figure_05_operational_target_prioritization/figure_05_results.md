A rule-based prioritization framework placed the L-P polymerase module at the front of the workflow and the prefusion F pocket in the orthogonal secondary position. L ranked first because it combined catalytic centrality, a drug-engaged structural template, and an established nonnucleoside inhibitor reference, whereas F ranked second because it provided a highly conserved and structurally validated entry pocket without the same catalytic leverage as the replication machinery. H and N were retained as biologically informative contextual modules rather than first-pass docking targets. This ranking translated the conservation and structural evidence into a defensible docking and molecular-dynamics sequence.
