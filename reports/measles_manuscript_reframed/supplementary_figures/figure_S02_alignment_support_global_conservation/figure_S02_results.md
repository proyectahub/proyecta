Alignment-level visualization corroborated the high conservation of both druggable measles virus pockets. Across 232 curated F sequences, nearly all rows were identical at the 13 pocket residues, with only sparse deviations at positions 453 and 458. Across 469 curated L sequences, the 31 ERDRP-0519-contact residues were likewise overwhelmingly conserved, with minor deviations restricted to positions 535, 544, and 739. The persistence of near-uniform residue-class patterns across both Latin American and non-Latin American rows confirms that the main-text conservation signal is not an artifact of summary statistics alone, but is directly evident in the underlying multiple sequence alignments.
