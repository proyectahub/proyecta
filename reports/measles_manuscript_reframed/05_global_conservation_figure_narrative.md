# Pillar I: Global Conservation of Druggable Pockets

## Scope

This pillar carries the mechanistic core of the manuscript. Its purpose is to establish that the main measles virus pockets selected for therapeutic exploration are not merely structurally visible, but conserved across a sufficiently broad regional-global sequence framework to justify a repurposing-oriented docking campaign.

Figure 1 remains the epidemiologic opener for the paper, but it functions as contextual motivation. The first figure belonging to the global-conservation pillar is therefore the reframed Figure 2.

## Order of Appearance

1. Figure 2. Public sequence evidence supports a regional-global analytic design for phylogeny and docking-oriented conservation.
2. Figure 3. Structure-guided landscape of the measles virus therapeutic target space.
3. Figure 4. Global conservation of docking-relevant measles virus F and L pockets.
4. Figure 5. Operational prioritization of F and L targets for docking and molecular dynamics.
5. Figure 6. Therapeutic repurposing framework and comparator strategy for the F and L campaigns.

## Narrative Logic

The logic should remain strictly cumulative:

1. define why target inference cannot rely on Mexican sequence density alone,
2. show which viral proteins are structurally actionable,
3. demonstrate that the key pockets are globally conserved,
4. convert that evidence into target priority,
5. translate target priority into a repurposing workflow.

## Figure-by-Figure Narrative

### Figure 2

**Narrative function**

This figure defines the evidence boundary of the sequence analysis. It explains why the paper cannot make strong therapeutic claims from a Mexico-only molecular framework and why a curated Latin American plus global comparison is required.

**Results paragraph**

Exact curation of GenBank source metadata showed that the public Mexican measles virus sequence space remained sparse and historically skewed. Only 10 unique Mexican N-gene records were retained after exact geographic filtering, whereas no Mexican F sequences and no Mexican L sequences were available in the curated dataset. By contrast, Latin American exact records yielded 138 N sequences, 10 F sequences, and 14 L sequences, and the global complete-genome context contributed 405 N, 432 F, and 630 L sequences. The latest curated Mexican public record dated to 2019, indicating that the 2025-2026 outbreak was not represented by publicly retrievable Mexican genome-scale sequence data in the dataset fetched on June 6, 2026. These data justified a regional-global analytical design in which phylogenetic context remained regionally relevant, but docking-oriented conservation required broader sequence support.

**Transition to next figure**

Once the regional-global sequence framework is established, the next step is to identify which measles virus proteins are structurally suitable for therapeutic targeting within that evidence space.

### Figure 3

**Narrative function**

This figure resolves the therapeutic target landscape and narrows it from general viral biology to actionable small-molecule space. It is the bridge between sequence evidence and pocket conservation.

**Results paragraph**

Integration of viral function with public structural evidence resolved the measles virus target space into two intervention-relevant modules: an entry-facing axis centered on H and F, and a replication-facing axis centered on the polymerase machinery and nucleocapsid scaffold. Although H and F both participate in entry, only F currently provides a structurally explicit inhibitor-accessible pocket in a target-engaged prefusion conformation. Likewise, while N remains essential for genome encapsidation and replication, the strongest target-engaged structural evidence within the replication module is concentrated in ligand-bound polymerase assemblies that include ERDRP-0519. Taken together, these data converged on prefusion F and the L polymerase complex as the two leading axes for downstream therapeutic analysis.

**Transition to next figure**

After identifying F and L as the principal structure-guided targets, the next question is whether the specific pockets used for docking are conserved strongly enough across available sequence space to support a generalizable therapeutic strategy.

### Figure 4

**Narrative function**

This is the core figure of the pillar. It converts the sequence-to-structure framework into a defensible therapeutic claim by showing that the selected F and L pockets are globally stable.

**Results paragraph**

Pocket-directed conservation analysis demonstrated that both docking-relevant therapeutic sites remained highly conserved across the curated regional-global sequence panels. All 13 F-contact residues mapped from the 5YZC inhibitor-bound prefusion structure remained at or above 98.57% consensus frequency, with only residues 453 and 458 showing minor variability. Likewise, all 31 L-contact residues mapped from the ERDRP-0519-bound 9OCF polymerase structure remained at or above 98.92% consensus frequency, and only residues 535, 544, and 739 fell below complete invariance. Structural superposition further supported the use of ligand-engaged templates, because 5YZC and 5YZD aligned at 0.2933 angstrom RMSD and 9OCF and 9OCE aligned at 0.1170 angstrom RMSD, whereas the ATP-bound 9OCH conformation was markedly displaced from the ERDRP-0519-bound state. These findings show that the selected F and L pockets are both structurally explicit and exceptionally conserved across the available regional-global measles virus sequence landscape.

**Transition to next figure**

Once conservation has been demonstrated, the remaining task is to convert conservation and structural evidence into an operational ranking that can govern the docking and molecular-dynamics workflow.

### Figure 5

**Narrative function**

This figure translates biology, structure, and conservation into computational decision-making. It explains why L is first and F is second.

**Results paragraph**

A transparent prioritization rubric integrating essential viral role, structural tractability, target-engaged complexes, and published antiviral mechanism placed the L polymerase pocket at the front of the computational workflow and the prefusion F pocket in the orthogonal secondary position. L ranked highest because it combines catalytic centrality with a drug-engaged structural template and an established nonnucleoside inhibitor reference. F ranked second because it offers a structurally defined and highly conserved entry pocket, but without the same catalytic leverage as the polymerase machinery. Under this framework, H and N were retained as contextual biological modules rather than first-pass docking targets. The prioritization therefore translated directly into a two-arm campaign in which L defines the primary docking axis and F defines the secondary orthogonal axis.

**Transition to next figure**

With the target order established, the next step is to define how a repurposing campaign should be built without conflating translational candidates with target-validation controls.

### Figure 6

**Narrative function**

This figure converts target priority into a screening design. It gives the translational endpoint of the global-conservation pillar.

**Results paragraph**

A tiered compound-selection strategy was implemented to separate clinically interpretable repurposing candidates from structural comparators used for pocket validation. The L arm was organized around a translational screening set of small molecules with established clinical exposure and mechanistic relevance to antiviral therapy, while ERDRP-0519 was retained as a direct polymerase benchmark rather than a repurposing lead. The F arm was framed more conservatively, using AS-48 and FIP as structural comparators that define and validate the prefusion entry pocket before broader screening expansion. This separation is methodologically important because it preserves a clean distinction between compounds intended to test therapeutic transferability and compounds intended to verify target recognition, pose plausibility, and pocket engagement.

**Transition beyond the pillar**

At this point, the manuscript can move naturally from conservation-based target definition into docking execution, benchmarking, and dynamic stability assessment.

## Recommended Main-Text Flow

The `Results` section should present this pillar in the following compact progression:

1. sequence evidence constrained the geographic scope of inference,
2. structural biology narrowed the target space to F and L,
3. pocket conservation justified a globally framed therapeutic claim,
4. prioritization converted that claim into target order,
5. repurposing logic converted target order into a screening strategy.

## Recommended Topic Sentence

The manuscript should introduce this pillar with a sentence of this form:

`Because recent outbreak relevance did not coincide with dense local sequence deposition, the therapeutic analysis was recentered on the identification of globally conserved measles virus pockets that remain structurally actionable across broader regional and global sequence space.`
