## Global Conservation of Druggable Pockets

Exact curation of GenBank source metadata showed that the public Mexican measles virus sequence space remained sparse and historically skewed, precluding a robust therapeutic analysis anchored in local sequence density alone. Only 10 unique Mexican N-gene records were retained after exact geographic filtering, whereas no Mexican F sequences and no Mexican L sequences were available in the curated dataset. By contrast, Latin American exact records yielded 138 N sequences, 10 F sequences, and 14 L sequences, and the global complete-genome context contributed 405 N, 432 F, and 630 L sequences. The latest curated Mexican public record dated to 2019, indicating that the 2025-2026 outbreak was not represented by publicly retrievable Mexican genome-scale sequence data in the dataset fetched on June 6, 2026. These observations justified a regional-global analytical design in which outbreak relevance remained regional, but docking-oriented conservation required broader sequence support.

Within that sequence framework, integration of viral biology with public structural evidence resolved the measles virus therapeutic space into an entry-facing module and a replication-facing module. Although H and F both contribute to cell entry, only F currently provides a structurally explicit small-molecule-accessible pocket in a target-engaged prefusion conformation. Likewise, while N remains essential to genome encapsidation and replication, the strongest target-engaged structural evidence within the replication module is concentrated in ligand-bound polymerase assemblies that include ERDRP-0519. These data converged on prefusion F and the L polymerase complex as the two leading structure-guided targets for downstream therapeutic exploration.

Pocket-directed conservation analysis then showed that both selected docking sites were highly stable across the curated regional-global sequence panels. All 13 F-contact residues mapped from the 5YZC inhibitor-bound prefusion structure remained at or above 98.57% consensus frequency, with only residues 453 and 458 showing minor variability. Similarly, all 31 L-contact residues mapped from the ERDRP-0519-bound 9OCF polymerase structure remained at or above 98.92% consensus frequency, with only residues 535, 544, and 739 falling below complete invariance. Structural superposition supported the use of ligand-engaged templates, because 5YZC and 5YZD aligned at 0.2933 angstrom RMSD and 9OCF and 9OCE aligned at 0.1170 angstrom RMSD, whereas the ATP-bound 9OCH state was markedly displaced relative to the ERDRP-0519-bound conformation. Together, these findings indicate that the selected F and L pockets are not only structurally explicit, but exceptionally conserved across the available measles virus sequence landscape.

A transparent prioritization rubric integrating essential viral role, structural tractability, target-engaged complexes, and published antiviral mechanism placed the L polymerase pocket at the front of the computational workflow and the prefusion F pocket in the orthogonal secondary position. L ranked first because it uniquely combined catalytic centrality with a drug-engaged structural template and an established nonnucleoside inhibitor reference. F ranked second because it provided a highly conserved and structurally validated entry pocket, although without the same catalytic leverage as the polymerase machinery. Under this framework, H and N were retained as biologically informative contextual modules rather than first-pass docking targets.

This ordering directly informed the repurposing strategy. The L arm was organized around a translational screening set of clinically interpretable small molecules, while ERDRP-0519 was retained as a direct polymerase benchmark rather than a repurposing lead. The F arm was framed more conservatively, using AS-48 and FIP as structural comparators that define and validate the prefusion inhibitor cavity before broader exploratory screening. Accordingly, the central therapeutic conclusion of this study is not that Mexico harbors a unique antiviral target profile, but that measles virus contains globally conserved, structurally actionable pockets that can support a rational repurposing framework with immediate relevance to regional outbreak preparedness.
