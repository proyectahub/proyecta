Pocket-directed conservation analysis showed that the measles F inhibitor cavity and the measles L ERDRP-0519 cavity were both highly conserved across the curated Latin America plus global sequence panels. All 13 F-contact residues identified from 5YZC remained at or above 98.57% consensus frequency, with only positions 453 and 458 showing minor variability. Likewise, all 31 L-contact residues derived from 9OCF remained at or above 98.92% consensus frequency, and only positions 535, 544, and 739 fell below complete invariance. Structural superposition further supported the use of ligand-engaged reference states for docking: 5YZC and 5YZD aligned at 0.2933 Å RMSD, and 9OCF and 9OCE aligned at 0.1170 Å RMSD, whereas the ATP-bound 9OCH state was markedly displaced relative to the ERDRP-0519-bound conformation. Together, these results indicate that the selected F and L pockets are not only structurally defined but also exceptionally conserved across the sequence space available for regional and global comparison.
