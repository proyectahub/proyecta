A tiered compound-selection strategy was implemented to prevent conflation of clinically oriented repurposing candidates with target-specific structural controls. The primary repurposing set was anchored to small molecules with public chemistry identifiers in ChEMBL and PubChem and with mechanistic relevance to viral RNA synthesis or broad antiviral activity. Under this rule set, remdesivir, favipiravir, and ribavirin were assigned to the L-P arm, whereas ERDRP-0519 was retained separately as a direct MeV polymerase benchmark rather than as a repurposing lead. The F arm was intentionally framed more conservatively, using AS-48 and FIP as pocket validators that define the prefusion entry site before any broader exploratory compound expansion. This separation is methodologically important because the repurposing shortlist is interpreted as a translational screening set, whereas ERDRP-0519, AS-48, and FIP are used to test pocket recognition, pose recovery, and target-specific plausibility.
