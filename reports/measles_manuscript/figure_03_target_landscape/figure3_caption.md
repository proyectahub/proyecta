Figure 3. Biologic and structural landscape of measles virus targets considered for therapeutic repurposing. (A) Simplified biologic map linking receptor attachment by H, membrane fusion by F, and RNA synthesis by the L-P machinery, with the nucleocapsid protein N serving as the encapsidated RNA scaffold that supports polymerase function. (B) Representative public MeV structural states used to assess target readiness. H is supported by receptor-bound entry complexes (3INB, 3ALZ, 4GJT), F by prefusion and inhibitor-bound structures (5YXW, 5YZC, 5YZD), N by scaffold states (6H5Q, 4UFT), and L-P by polymerase assemblies including ERDRP-0519-bound complexes (9DUT, 9OCE, 9OCF). The strongest direct structural bridge to small-molecule intervention is provided by the drug-engaged MeV polymerase complexes, followed by inhibitor-defined prefusion F states.
