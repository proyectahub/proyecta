Integration of biologic function with public structural evidence resolved the measles virus target space into an entry tier and a replication tier. H and F define the entry axis, but the nature of their structural support differs. H is currently represented mainly by receptor-engaged complexes that capture CD46-, SLAM-, and NECTIN4-facing attachment states, whereas F is represented by prefusion and inhibitor-bound conformations that expose a discrete entry pocket. In parallel, the replication axis is defined by the encapsidated RNA scaffold provided by N and by the catalytic RNA-synthesis machinery formed by L and P. The most intervention-ready structural evidence was concentrated in the polymerase tier, where recent cryo-EM MeV assemblies include ERDRP-0519-bound holo complexes that directly connect structure, pocket definition, and antiviral mechanism. Taken together, these data place polymerase-centered targeting at the front of the therapeutic workflow, while supporting the prefusion F pocket as the leading orthogonal entry-focused target.
