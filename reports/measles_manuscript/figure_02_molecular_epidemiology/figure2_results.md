The molecular epidemiology of measles in Mexico was consistent with repeated importation events rather than re-establishment of a single endemic lineage. The federal bulletin released on 4 April 2025 documented genotype detections spanning D4 in 2011, D8 in 2013, 2020, and 2024, B3 in 2014 and 2018, D9 in 2015, and co-detection of B3 and D8 in 2019 and again in 2025. The nationwide 2025-2026 outbreak analysis refined this pattern by identifying two independent introductions, with D8/MVs/Ontario.CAN/47.24 accounting for 98.1% of typed outbreak sequences and B3 accounting for 1.9% in Oaxaca. A current public-sequence audit further indicated that molecular surveillance remains underrepresented in easily retrievable public records. The broad nuccore query returned 94 hits on 5 June 2026, but only 22 Mexico-associated N-gene entries remained after exclusion of New Mexico/USA false positives, and only 18 entries met a strict Mexican-source audit based on explicit `.MEX` strain labels or non-foreign Mexico source metadata. These strict records were entirely historical and clustered between 2003 and 2019. In parallel, the outbreak paper reported 26 historical N-450 sequences from Mexico for 2003-2021, indicating that Mexico's molecular outbreak complexity is not matched by the accessibility and consistency of public accession metadata.
