Figure 2. Molecular epidemiology of measles virus circulation in Mexico and the public sequencing gap. (A) Official genotype detections compiled from the Mexican Ministry of Health bulletin No. 4 (4 April 2025), showing repeated detections of D4, D8, B3, and D9 rather than persistence of a single lineage. (B) Lineage composition reported in the nationwide 2025-2026 outbreak analysis, which identified two independent introductions: D8/MVs/Ontario.CAN/47.24 (98.1%) and B3 (1.9%) in Oaxaca. (C) Strict current nuccore audit of publicly retrievable Mexican N-gene accessions, aggregated by collection year and genotype. Only 18 strict Mexican records were recovered, all historical and concentrated between 2003 and 2019. (D) Audit summary for the broad nuccore query `"measles virus"[All Fields] AND Mexico[All Fields]` executed on 5 June 2026. The nationwide outbreak analysis reported 26 historical N-450 sequences from Mexico (2003-2021), whereas the current accession audit recovered 22 Mexico-associated N-gene entries after removal of New Mexico/USA false positives and 18 strict Mexican N-gene records carrying explicit `.MEX` or non-foreign Mexico source metadata.
