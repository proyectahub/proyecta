Figure 3. Structure-guided prioritization of measles virus therapeutic targets for docking and molecular dynamics. (A) Therapeutic landscape linking receptor attachment (H), membrane fusion (F), and the L-P-N replication machinery. (B) Representative public MeV structures selected from RCSB PDB to justify target readiness, including receptor-bound H complexes (3INB, 3ALZ, 4GJT), prefusion and inhibitor-bound F structures (5YXW, 5YZC, 5YZD), nucleocapsid scaffold structures (6H5Q, 4UFT), and recent MeV polymerase assemblies including ERDRP-0519-bound holo complexes (9DUT, 9OCE, 9OCF). (C) Rule-based study score integrating five evidence layers: essential viral role, catalytic machinery, public high-resolution structure, target-engaged MeV complex, and published MeV antiviral mechanism. This rubric ranked the L-P polymerase machinery highest, followed by F, with H retained for biologic context and N deferred. (D) Study deployment derived from this prioritization: Mexican lineage context informs sequence-aware model selection, the primary docking/MD campaign is centered on L-P, the secondary campaign on F, and H is preserved for receptor-facing interpretation.
