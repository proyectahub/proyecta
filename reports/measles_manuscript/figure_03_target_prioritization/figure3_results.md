Structure-guided triage identified the measles virus polymerase machinery as the strongest initial target for computational repurposing. UniProt-supported functional annotation separated entry proteins from replication proteins, with H mediating receptor attachment, F directing membrane fusion, and L-P-N supporting transcription and replication. Public structural coverage was deepest for three target classes. The attachment axis was represented by H complexes bound to CD46, SLAM, and NECTIN4; the entry-fusion axis by prefusion and inhibitor-bound F structures; and the replication axis by recent cryo-EM MeV polymerase assemblies, including ERDRP-0519-bound holo complexes. When these evidence layers were integrated in a transparent five-component scoring rubric, L-P ranked highest because it combines an essential catalytic role, high-resolution target-engaged structures, and a direct MeV antiviral mechanism defined for ERDRP-0519. F ranked second because its inhibitor-bound prefusion pocket is structurally explicit and mechanistically validated, whereas H was retained as a contextual target for lineage-aware receptor interpretation rather than as the primary small-molecule docking axis. This analysis established the operational order for the computational section: L-P first, F second, H contextual, and N deferred.
