A steep escalation of the measles outbreak in Mexico was documented in official surveillance releases during 2025. The national confirmed count increased from 22 cases in the federal advisory released on 12 March 2025 to 126 cases in Bulletin No. 4 on 4 April 2025. WHO/PAHO subsequently documented 421 confirmed Mexican cases by mid-April (published 28 April 2025), with transmission already distributed across eight states and one reported death. By 23 May 2025, the Ministry of Health daily report documented 1,727 cumulative confirmed cases, four deaths, and confirmed transmission in 17 states and 56 municipalities. The regional situation worsened further in 2026, when PAHO reported 10,920 confirmed Mexican cases and 13 deaths by epidemiological week 19, with cases present in 31 federative entities. In parallel, national MMR coverage in 2025 remained below the recommended two-dose threshold, reaching 86.7% for the first dose and 84.4% for the second dose. Taken together, these official data support the interpretation that the outbreak progressed from a focal event to widespread national transmission in the setting of incomplete population immunity.
