After exact filtering by GenBank source metadata rather than free-text query matches, the public Mexican sequence space remained sparse and historically skewed. Only 10 unique Mexican N-gene records were retained for downstream phylogenetic analysis, whereas no Mexican F sequences and no Mexican L sequences were available in the curated set. By contrast, Latin American exact records yielded 138 N sequences, 10 F sequences, and 14 L sequences, while the global complete-genome context contributed 405 N, 432 F, and 630 L sequences. The latest curated Mexican public record dated to 2019, indicating that no publicly deposited Mexican sequences from the 2025-2026 outbreak were recovered in GenBank on June 6, 2026. This data structure justified a split analytic design in which N supported regional phylogenetic reconstruction and F/L supported amino-acid conservation mapping onto docking-relevant structural pockets.
