A transparent five-component prioritization rubric was applied uniformly across candidate targets, integrating essential viral role, catalytic machinery, public high-resolution structure, target-engaged MeV complex, and published direct antiviral mechanism. Under this framework, L-P received the highest score because it uniquely combines catalytic centrality with high-resolution drug-engaged polymerase structures and a mechanistically defined nonnucleoside inhibitor reference. F ranked second because its prefusion pocket is structurally explicit and experimentally validated, but it does not offer the same catalytic leverage as the replication machinery. H remained biologically informative but was reassigned to a contextual module because the current evidence set supports receptor interpretation more strongly than lead small-molecule deployment, whereas N was retained as a deferred scaffold module. This triage translated directly into the computational workflow: L-P was assigned to the primary docking and molecular dynamics campaign, F to the secondary orthogonal campaign, and H and N to interpretation and later expansion rather than first-pass screening.
