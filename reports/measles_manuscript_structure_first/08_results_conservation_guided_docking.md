## Conservation-guided refinement resolved a narrower invariant core within the ERDRP-0519 pocket

The sequence-conservation analysis did not merely support the choice of the `9OCF` polymerase pocket at the level of the full contact shell; it also resolved a more restrictive invariant core suitable for refinement docking. Of the 31 residues within `6 A` of ERDRP-0519 in the holo structure, 15 satisfied both direct-contact and complete-conservation criteria, namely `LEU664`, `TYR667`, `TRP671`, `GLU740`, `GLY741`, `GLN744`, `TRP747`, `THR751`, `LEU755`, `GLY772`, `ASP773`, `GLN775`, `LEU811`, `HIS816`, and `LEU818`. Each of these positions remained at a consensus frequency of `1.0` across the curated non-gap `L` alignment, indicating that the direct pharmacophore core of the ERDRP-0519 site was fully invariant in the available regional-global sequence space.

By contrast, the few contact-shell residues that fell below complete invariance were peripheral to this direct-contact core. Only `K535`, `R544`, and `I739` dropped below a consensus frequency of `1.0`, and all three lay outside the `<=4 A` conserved core used to define the refinement geometry. This pattern indicates that the minor sequence variability present in the polymerase contact shell is not centered on the densest ligand-engaged interaction cluster. Accordingly, the original holo-ligand box remains appropriate for first-pass discovery, but a second, more restrictive box can be justified for downstream refinement without sacrificing alignment consistency.

The resulting refinement box was smaller and slightly shifted relative to the discovery box derived from the full ERDRP-0519 pose. Whereas the initial first-pass search region was centered at `(170.516, 167.880, 187.170)` with dimensions `21.099 x 13.600 x 24.173 A`, the conserved-core refinement box was centered at `(172.236, 168.128, 188.918)` with dimensions `17.033 x 12.000 x 17.607 A`. This conservation-guided contraction preserves the experimentally validated pocket identity while focusing subsequent redocking or rescoring on the invariant structural nucleus most directly supported by the sequence alignment.
