# Structure-First Positioning

## Why the alignment figure should be main text

The alignment-support figure was previously placed in the supplementary section for an editorial reason: the manuscript had been organized around outbreak relevance first and therapeutic inference second. Under that design, the multiple-sequence-alignment layer functioned as a support artifact for the pocket-conservation claim.

That logic is no longer optimal if the study is to be centered on the intrinsic properties of the virus. If the main scientific question is how to rationalize docking toward measles virus sites that are both structurally actionable and highly conserved, then the alignment figure is not auxiliary evidence. It is part of the core inferential chain.

## Recommended scientific axis

The manuscript should be re-centered on the following sequence:

1. identify structurally actionable viral targets,
2. show that the relevant pocket residues are conserved in sequence space,
3. demonstrate that this conservation is directly visible in the underlying alignments,
4. prioritize the targets for docking,
5. build the repurposing workflow on top of that target order,
6. leave regional epidemiology as background context rather than as the organizing logic.

## Working title

Structure-guided identification of highly conserved measles virus druggable pockets for rational antiviral repurposing

## Central hypothesis

Measles virus preserves structurally explicit small-molecule pockets in prefusion F and the L polymerase machinery that remain highly conserved across available sequence space and therefore provide a rational foundation for docking-driven antiviral repurposing.

## Practical implication

Under this structure-first framing, regional epidemiology no longer needs to open the Results section. It can remain in the Introduction or move to supplementary context, while the main narrative begins with viral target architecture and alignment-supported conservation.
