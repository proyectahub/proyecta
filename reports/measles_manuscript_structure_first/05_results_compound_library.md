## Compound library selection results

The current DrugCentral FDA registry contained `2331` approved entries, of which `1876` had a directly recoverable structure in the DrugCentral 2023 structural archive after standardization. Cross-annotation against ChEMBL recovered a large corroborated fraction of the FDA-approved chemical universe, with `1612` compounds supported by both databases. After exclusion of isotopically labeled molecules and compounds containing nonclassical elements, followed by soft physicochemical filtering and PAINS exclusion, the first-pass FDA virtual screening library retained `1260` dockable compounds. Requiring additional ChEMBL support yielded a high-confidence subset of `1165` compounds. This high-confidence library was then partitioned into `1165` molecules for the `L` polymerase campaign and `1020` molecules for the more restrictive `F` pocket campaign. Compounds carrying Brenk alerts were retained within the primary screen but tagged as lower-priority entries for downstream interpretation.
