## Conservation-guided refinement of the L polymerase docking pocket

The first-pass `L` polymerase docking campaign was anchored to the ERDRP-0519-bound `9OCF` holo structure and initially used the full ligand-derived search box as an experimentally grounded discovery region. To align the downstream refinement stage with the sequence analysis, the 31 residues located within 6 A of ERDRP-0519 were intersected with the pocket-level conservation profile derived from the curated regional-global `L` alignment. Residues were retained in the conserved structural core only when they satisfied both geometric proximity and complete conservation criteria, defined as a minimum ligand-contact distance of `<=4 A` in `9OCF` and a consensus frequency of `1.0` across the non-gap alignment positions. This yielded a 15-residue conserved core comprising `LEU664`, `TYR667`, `TRP671`, `GLU740`, `GLY741`, `GLN744`, `TRP747`, `THR751`, `LEU755`, `GLY772`, `ASP773`, `GLN775`, `LEU811`, `HIS816`, and `LEU818`.

To derive a stricter refinement box, ERDRP-0519 atoms located within `4 A` of this conserved core were extracted from the reference ligand pose. The centroid and axis-aligned span of these core-contacting ligand atoms were then computed, and each axis was expanded by a `3 A` margin per side, with a minimum edge length of `12 A`, to preserve local search flexibility without reverting to the broader first-pass search volume. Under this rule, the refinement box center was placed at `(172.236, 168.128, 188.918)` with final dimensions of `17.033 x 12.000 x 17.607 A`. The broader first-pass box was retained for the initial virtual screen, whereas the conserved-core box was reserved for redocking, rescoring, or later refinement of prioritized hits.
