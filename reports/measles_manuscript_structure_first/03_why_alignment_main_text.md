# Why the Alignment Figure Belongs in the Main Text

## Scientific reason

The alignment figure is not merely decorative confirmation of the conservation tables. It answers a different question:

- the pocket-conservation figure asks `how conserved are the selected residues?`
- the alignment-support figure asks `is that conservation actually visible in the underlying sequence data?`

For a docking-driven paper, both questions are central.

## Why this matters for docking

Docking should not be directed toward a site only because a structure exists. It should be directed toward a site where the residue architecture of the pocket remains stable across the sequence space that defines the virus. The alignment figure is therefore part of the target-rationalization chain:

1. target exists structurally,
2. residues are conserved statistically,
3. residues remain visibly stable in the alignment,
4. the site is justified for docking.

## Editorial implication

If the manuscript opens with viral properties and target logic, then the alignment-support figure should appear before or alongside the pocket-level conservation figure. Under that logic, placing it in supplementary material weakens the argument rather than streamlining it.

## Recommended use

The best structure-first sequence is:

1. target landscape,
2. alignment-support figure,
3. pocket-conservation figure,
4. prioritization figure,
5. repurposing figure.
