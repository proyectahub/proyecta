Ligand selection followed a monotonic reduction from regulatory approval space to a docking-ready chemical subset. Starting from `2,331` DrugCentral FDA-approved entries, only `1,876` compounds had a directly recoverable parent structure in the DrugCentral 2023 structural archive. Removal of isotopically labeled compounds and molecules containing nonclassical elements produced `1,841` chemically classical parent structures. Application of the docking-oriented physicochemical envelope together with PAINS exclusion retained `1,260` compounds in the primary FDA virtual-screening set. Restricting this set to compounds independently represented in ChEMBL `max_phase = 4` yielded a high-confidence subset of `1,165` molecules. This library was carried forward in full for the `L` polymerase campaign, whereas the more restrictive `F` pocket campaign retained `1,020` compounds. Within the `L` arm, `692` compounds formed the clean first tier and `473` additional compounds were retained as a Brenk-flag lower-priority tier rather than being discarded outright.
