Figure 6. Funnel-based assembly of the FDA-approved ligand library for measles virtual screening.

The figure shows the staged reduction from the DrugCentral FDA-approved regulatory universe to a docking-oriented high-confidence library. Only recoverable parent structures were retained, after which isotopic labels and compounds with nonclassical elements were removed. The primary virtual-screening subset was then defined by a soft physicochemical envelope and PAINS exclusion. A final corroboration step retained compounds also represented in ChEMBL `max_phase = 4` small molecules. The resulting high-confidence library was routed to the `L` polymerase campaign and the more restrictive `F` prefusion campaign, with the `L` arm further separated into a clean tier and a Brenk-flag lower-priority tier.
