The prefusion F pocket also resolved into a narrower invariant structural core suitable for refinement docking. Of the 13 residues within 6 A of AS-48 in 5YZC, seven satisfied both direct-contact and complete-conservation criteria: TRP311, THR369, LEU370, PRO451, ILE452, VAL459, and ASN462. All AS-48 atoms remained within 4 A of this invariant core, indicating that the experimentally observed ligand sits almost entirely within the sequence-supported pharmacophore nucleus. The only pocket residues below complete invariance were S453 and D458, which therefore define a peripheral variability layer rather than the central binding determinant.

Under this rule, the first-pass F box centered on the full AS-48 pose was contracted into a more restrictive conserved-core refinement box with the same center but smaller edge lengths. This provides an F-side analogue to the L polymerase refinement strategy: the initial exploratory screen can use the broader ligand-defined pocket, whereas subsequent redocking or rescoring can be focused on the invariant entry-pocket core most directly supported by the alignment.
