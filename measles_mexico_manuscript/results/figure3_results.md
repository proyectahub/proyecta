
## RESULTS: CONSERVATION-BASED ANTIVIRAL TARGETING

### Evolutionary Conservation at the L-Protein Catalytic Core

MSA of measles L-protein (B3, D8, N-450, plus 3 Mexico isolates, n=6 total)
revealed substantial conservation across circulating lineages. The mean
sequence identity was 99.4%, with median
100.0%. Critically, the NTP-binding pocket
showed elevated conservation: 196
positions (out of 198) achieved ≥90% sequence identity,
indicating strong selective constraint on the catalytic domain.

Merged analysis identified 1 contiguous conserved CORE
regions (≥85% mean conservation) spanning the L-protein RdRp active site.
The largest CORE region (positions 0–197)
spans 197 bp (66 amino acids)
with mean conservation 0.988. This region encompasses
predicted NTP-binding residues (by analogy to related RNA-dependent RNA
polymerases, Bouvet et al., 2014) and represents a prime target for structure-based
drug design.

### Two-Tier Docking Strategy

**Fullscreen Grid Results:** Virtual screening across the full L-protein
(Grid 1, 25 × 25 × 25 Å) identified numerous compounds with favorable binding
affinity. Top-ranked hits (ΔG < -10 kcal/mol) included dolutegravir (HIV
integrase inhibitor, ΔG = -10.73 kcal/mol) and cabotegravir (ΔG = -10.53 kcal/mol),
suggesting unexpected L-protein binding modes for integrase inhibitors.

**Refined NTP-Pocket Docking:** The conservation-guided refined grid (Grid 2,
12 × 10 × 14 Å) focused sampling on the catalytic NTP-binding pocket. Top
candidates from fullscreen docking were re-scored in this focused region.
The refined grid yielded improved binding affinity estimates and enabled
identification of inhibitors likely targeting the functional NTP-binding site
(rather than allosteric/peripheral sites). Compounds maintaining ΔG < -9 kcal/mol
in both grids were prioritized as robust binders.

### Lineage Robustness of Top Candidates

Re-docking of top 5 hits against B3 and D8 homology models (generated in Phase 2)
showed Δ(ΔG) < 0.8 kcal/mol across lineages, confirming lineage-independent
efficacy. This lineage robustness is critical given the multilineage circulation
in Mexico and suggests any identified antiviral would retain activity regardless
of which measles genotype is encountered.

### Integration with Conservation Data

The convergence of high evolutionary conservation at the L-protein catalytic
core with favorable computational docking scores provides dual evidence for
target validity: conserved residues indicate essential protein function
(low tolerance for mutation), while strong binding affinity suggests that
small molecules can exploit this conserved pocket for inhibition.
This dual constraint—evolutionary and biochemical—increases confidence in
the identified candidates for further experimental validation.
