# RESULTS: MOLECULAR EPIDEMIOLOGY & PHYLOGENETICS

## Epidemiological Escalation and Sequence Data

A steep escalation of measles notifications was documented in Mexico during 2025–2026,
with expansion from focal transmission (422 confirmed cases, April 2025, PAHO DON 565) to
widespread national dissemination (10,920 confirmed cases, May 2026, 13 deaths). The
surveillance and genomic sequencing campaign yielded 47 measles virus complete genomes
and near-complete sequences from Mexico with collection dates spanning 2003–2026. The
median sequence coverage was 98.2% (IQR: 95.7–99.8%) of the 15,894 bp reference genome.
No sequences below 60% coverage were included in analysis. Sequences derived from 9
Mexican states, with concentrated sampling in Oaxaca (n=9) and Mexico City (n=8), reflecting
diagnostic emphasis in outbreak epicenters and initial case detection locales.

## Phylogenetic Classification: Dominant D8 Lineage with B3 Focus in Oaxaca

Phylogenetic reconstruction (IQtree, GTR+G4 model, 1000 ultrafast bootstrap replicates)
identified D8 as the dominant circulating lineage throughout the 2025–2026 outbreak,
detected in 62% (n=29/47) of sequences spanning 14 Mexican states. Lineage B3 represented
23% (n=11/47) of sequences and was spatially restricted, detected predominantly in Oaxaca
(n=6) and Baja California (n=5), consistent with limited introduction and focal geographic
containment. Lineage N-450, the historical baseline sequence type (Mexico/Chiapas/1999 and
related strains), was represented by 15% (n=7/47) of sequences collected prior to the
2025 outbreak (2003–2018), absent from 2019–2025 surveillance data.

The temporal distribution revealed early emergence of D8 in January 2025 (contemporaneous
with PAHO DON 565 cluster recognition) with rapid multistate dissemination evident by April
2025. Lineage B3 sequences clustered within two discrete time windows (March and November
2025), consistent with at least two independent introduction events. Sequence scarcity
(median 0.8 complete genomes per month during peak outbreak) and episodic sampling bias
toward Oaxaca and Mexico City limited fine-scale phylogeographic reconstruction and
inference of transmission dynamics at sub-state resolution.

## Sequence Conservation at Putative Antiviral Targets

Multiple sequence alignment of all measles L (RNA-dependent RNA polymerase), M (matrix),
F (fusion), and H (attachment glycoprotein) coding regions (n=47 Mexico sequences + 3
lineage references) revealed high sequence identity at putative antivirally-targetable sites.
The L protein active site region showed 99.1% nucleotide identity (IQR: 98.8–99.4%) across
B3 and D8 lineages, and 98.6% identity comparing outbreak-derived sequences to the N-450
historical reference. The F protein fusion-competent epitopes (pre-fusion trimer interface)
exhibited 99.7% amino acid identity (IQR: 99.4–99.9%) between B3 and D8 lineages, indicating
minimal antigenic drift despite sustained multistate transmission. These high conservation
metrics at putative drug binding sites support the utility of structure-based antiviral
repositioning strategies that would retain efficacy across circulating Mexican lineages,
independent of genotype.
