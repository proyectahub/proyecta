### Full Version (250+ words for journal)

> **Figure 3. Conservation-Guided Refinement of the Measles L-Protein Binding Pocket.** (A) Crystal structure of the measles L-polymerase (RdRp) binding pocket (PDB 9OCF, chain A) displayed as a cartoon ribbon in gray. Conservation-guided residue selection from multiple sequence alignment of B3, D8, N-450, and Mexico 2025–2026 measles lineages (n=28) identified 15 core conserved residues (blue spheres, conservation frequency ≥0.90) forming the NTP-binding pocket catalytic core, and 3 variable shell residues (orange spheres, conservation frequency <0.90) marking the minor-variable periphery. Mean conservation across the alignment: 99.4%. (B) Quantitative analysis of per-residue conservation frequency across the binding pocket region (positions 0–200). Blue bars represent positions with conservation frequency exceeding the 90% threshold (196/198 positions, 99.0% of the pocket). Orange dashed line indicates 85% conservation level; red dashed line indicates 90% conservation threshold. This highly constrained sequence conservation profile provides strong justification for focused antiviral targeting to this core region, as mutations outside this consensus sequence may be deleterious to viral fitness. (C) Docking grid refinement strategy comparing the initial broad-spectrum screening grid (25×25×25 Å = 15,625 Å³, light blue box) with the conservation-guided refined grid (12×10×14 Å = 1,680 Å³, dark blue box). Three orthogonal projections (X-Y, X-Z, Y-Z) visualize spatial positioning, and accompanying bar charts quantify dimension reduction. The refined grid achieves 9.3-fold volume reduction, concentrating sampling on conservation-identified core residues and the validated NTP-binding site (center: 170.5, 167.9, 187.2 Å). This conservation-informed refinement strategy enables focused and computationally efficient docking of antiviral candidates to functionally validated binding sites.
